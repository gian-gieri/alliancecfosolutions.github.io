The System

Your calendar is your business. If it’s not on Calendly, it doesn’t happen. No “let me just call you real quick” — that’s how boundaries erode.

Meeting-by-Meeting Playbook

🟢 Free Intro Call (20 min)

This is your filter, not your sales pitch.

  • You’re deciding if THEY are worth 45 minutes — not convincing them to hire you
  • Keep it light: learn about their business, listen for pain signals, assess fit
  • If it’s a fit → “This sounds like a great conversation to continue. Let me send you my Discovery Session link so we can go deeper.”
  • If it’s NOT a fit → “I don’t think I’m the right person for this, but let me connect you with someone who might be.”
  • Never pitch pricing on an intro call. That’s what Discovery is for.
  • Pro tip: Batch these. Consider keeping Tues/Thurs 10am-12pm as your intro call windows once volume picks up. For now, keep your full 10-5 window open.

🔵 Discovery Session (45 min)

Only send this link AFTER a successful intro call. Never on your website.

  • Send your discovery call prep checklist 24hrs before so you walk in sharp
  • Follow the discovery call script (client-systems/discovery-call-script.md)
  • This is where the deal closes — diagnose, don’t pitch
  • End with: “I’ll put together a proposal and send it over by [day]. No pressure.”
  • Within 24 hours: send proposal + follow-up email

🔵 Client Strategy Call (45 min)

Set these as RECURRING when you onboard. Same day, same time, every month.

  • Don’t re-book monthly. A standing appointment makes them feel like they have a real CFO.
  • Send the financial package 48 hours before. Every time. No exceptions.
  • Follow the strategy call agenda template (monthly-delivery/strategy-call-agenda.md)
  • Send call summary email within 24 hours
  • The rhythm: Financial package → Call → Summary email → Action items → Repeat

🔵 Quick Check-in (15 min)

Train your clients to use this instead of random texts and calls.

When a client texts you a question: → “Great question — grab a 15-minute slot and let’s knock it out properly: [link]”

This does three things: 1. Protects your time from constant interruptions 2. Ensures you’re prepared (you’ll see the booking and can prep) 3. Creates a professional boundary without being cold

Upsell signal: If a client books more than 2 check-ins per month, they need more than their current tier. Time for the upgrade conversation.

🔵 Referral Partner Coffee (30 min)

Use this with every referral partner from your connection list.

The flow: 1. They accept your LinkedIn connection request 2. You message: “Thanks for connecting — I’d love to learn about your practice and how we might be able to refer business to each other. Here’s my coffee chat link if you’re up for it: [link]” 3. On the call: Ask about THEIR ideal client first. Then share yours. Look for overlap. 4. Follow up within 48 hours with a thank-you and any referrals you promised

Goal: Build 10 active referral partners who each send you 1 lead per quarter. That’s 40 warm leads per year. At a 25% close rate, that’s 10 new clients from referrals alone.

General Calendar Rules

Availability: M-F 10am-5pm MST. Protect mornings for deep work when you can.

Buffer: 15 minutes after every call. Use it to write notes, update CRM, send follow-ups. Not to scroll LinkedIn.

Minimum notice: 24 hours for everything except Quick Check-ins (4 hours). You need time to prep.

No back-and-forth scheduling. Ever. Send the Calendly link. If someone says “what times work for you?” → send the link. If they push back on using a scheduling tool → they’re probably not your client.

Block personal time first. School pickup, family dinner, Amber’s schedule — block these before anything else. Clients get what’s left, not the other way around.
