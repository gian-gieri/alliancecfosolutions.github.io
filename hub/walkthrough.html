The Big Picture — When You Use What

  1. Someone finds you (LinkedIn, website, referral) → They book a Free Intro Call via Calendly
  2. Intro call goes well → You use the Discovery Call Script, then send them the Discovery Session link
  3. Discovery call confirms fit → You fill out the Proposal Template (Word) and send it
  4. They say yes → You send the Engagement Letter (Word) for their tier
  5. They sign → You send the Welcome Email and the Onboarding Checklist
  6. Onboarding complete → You create their Client Folder, pull QBO data into the Cash Flow Forecast and KPI Dashboard (Excel)
  7. Every month → You use the Monthly Review Checklist, build the Financial Package (Excel), write the Cover Memo (Word), present with the Monthly Review Deck (PowerPoint)
  8. Every quarter → You run the QBR using the report (Word) and deck (PowerPoint)
  9. Always → Track leads in the Pipeline Tracker (Excel), follow up using the Follow-Up Cadence, nurture referral partners

Client Systems — Getting Clients From "Hello" to "Signed"

These files cover everything from the first phone call to the signed engagement letter. This is your sales infrastructure.

discovery-call-script.md

Use: Every discovery call

Your 45-minute conversation framework. Not a script you read from — a structure that keeps you on track. Includes:

  • Pre-call prep checklist (what to look up about them)
  • Opening line that establishes credibility without pitching
  • 7 core questions that uncover their real pain
  • How to transition from diagnosis to solution
  • Closing language that moves to proposal
  • Red flags that tell you they're NOT your client

proposal-template.md

Use: Within 24 hours after every discovery call

One-page proposal with fill-in-the-blank sections. Four versions (one per tier). Structure: The Situation (reflect their pain) → What I'll Do (specific deliverables) → How It Works (retainer details) → Investment (monthly fee) → Next Step. Designed to feel like you listened, not like a template.

engagement-letter-diagnostic.md

Use: When a new client signs at the $1,500/mo tier

60-day diagnostic engagement. Scope: financial assessment, gap analysis, cash flow snapshot, 2 strategy calls, written roadmap. Includes what's NOT included, payment terms (ACH auto-pay), scope creep clause ($250/hr for extras), confidentiality, and 30-day termination. Have your attorney review before first use.

engagement-letter-essential.md

Use: $2,500/mo tier

Same legal structure as Diagnostic but scoped for ongoing Essential engagement — monthly close review, financial package, 1 strategy call/month, 48hr email SLA.

engagement-letter-growth.md

Use: $5,000/mo tier

Everything in Essential plus 13-month rolling forecast, KPI dashboard, 2 calls/month, ad hoc access.

engagement-letter-strategic.md

Use: $7,500–$10,000/mo tier

Full fractional CFO — weekly check-ins, cash flow modeling, lender/investor prep, same-day response.

onboarding-checklist.md

Use: Immediately after engagement letter is signed

14 items you need from every new client: QBO access, 12 months bank statements, 2 years tax returns, current financials, AR/AP aging, debt schedule, org chart, insurance summary, entity docs, top 3 financial goals, bookkeeper contact, key contracts. Plus a 4-week onboarding timeline (Week 1: collect → Week 2: assess → Week 3: first deliverable → Week 4: first strategy call).

welcome-email.md

Use: Same day the engagement letter is signed

Warm, professional email that sets expectations for the first 30 days. Confirms their tier, outlines what happens next, links to onboarding checklist, includes Calendly link for first strategy call, sets communication expectations.

follow-up-emails.md

Use: After every discovery call and proposal

Three email templates by temperature: Hot Lead (excited, send proposal, clear CTA), Warm Lead (lighter touch, no pressure), Not a Fit (graceful pass, offer referral). Plus the chase sequence: Day 3 check-in, Day 7 gentle nudge, Day 14 final follow-up. Each one sounds human, not templated.

client-folder-template.md

Use: When onboarding a new client — create this folder structure

Standardized folder structure for every client: 01-Onboarding, 02-Financial-Statements (Monthly/Annual), 03-Cash-Flow, 04-KPI-Dashboard, 05-Strategy-Calls/Notes, 06-Tax-Planning, 07-Correspondence, 08-Reports-Deliverables. Same structure every time = nothing gets lost.

pricing-reference.md

Use: Internal only — before every pricing conversation

Quick reference card with all 4 tiers, deliverables, ideal client for each, upsell triggers (when to suggest moving up), discount policy (don't — but annual prepay = 1 month free if forced), scope creep response scripts (exact phrases), and competitor pricing context.

calendly-links.md

Use: Quick reference anytime you need a booking link

All 5 Calendly meeting types with direct links. Free Intro Call (public), Discovery Session, Client Strategy Call, Quick Check-in, Referral Partner Coffee (all private/secret).

calendly-best-practices.md

Use: Reference doc — how to use each meeting type efficiently

When to send which link, how to batch calls, how to protect your time, upsell signals (client booking too many check-ins = needs higher tier), and the golden rule: no scheduling outside Calendly.

Monthly Delivery — What Clients GET Each Month

This is your product. If this is dialed, clients stay forever and refer others. Every file here is something you use every single month for every single client.

monthly-review-checklist.md

Use: Week 2 of every month, for every client

Step-by-step checklist for reviewing a client's financials: verify reconciliation, review P&L (trends, margins, expenses), compare to prior month/year/budget, review balance sheet (cash, AR, AP, debt), check cash flow, calculate key ratios, update forecast, update KPI dashboard, prep talking points, flag urgent items.

financial-package-template.md

Use: Week 3 — this is what the client receives

The monthly deliverable structure: Cover memo (1 paragraph on what the numbers say), Executive Summary (3-5 bullets), P&L with commentary (not just numbers — what they MEAN), Balance Sheet highlights, Cash Flow snapshot, KPI scorecard vs targets, Cash flow forecast update, Action Items (specific, prioritized), Questions for the call.

strategy-call-agenda.md

Use: Week 4 — every strategy call follows this structure

45-minute agenda: Check-in (5 min), Financial review together (15 min), Deep dive on one strategic topic (15 min — examples: pricing, hiring, debt, expansion), Action items (5 min), Parking lot (5 min). Includes pre-call prep and post-call tasks.

call-summary-email.md

Use: Within 24 hours after every strategy call

Template: Date, key discussion points (3-5 bullets), decisions made, action items with owners and due dates, next call date. Quick to fill out, huge for client trust.

cash-flow-forecast-guide.md

Use: Reference — how to build and maintain the 13-month forecast

Complete guide for the rolling forecast: structure (months as columns, categories as rows), revenue by stream, COGS, fixed vs variable expenses, debt service, capex, distributions. How to update monthly (replace forecast with actuals, extend one month). Scenario toggles (best/base/worst). Red flag triggers.

quarterly-business-review.md

Use: Every 90 days — deeper strategic conversation

QBR template: Financial performance (quarter vs prior quarter vs prior year), progress against annual goals, cash flow trajectory, KPI trends (3-month view), strategic wins, areas of concern with recommended actions, next quarter priorities (top 3), 12-month outlook.

week-by-week-cadence.md

Use: Reference — your monthly rhythm for each client

Week 1: Books close, reconciliation. Week 2: You review, build package, update forecast. Week 3: Deliver package 48hrs before call. Week 4: Strategy call, summary email, action items. Includes notes on scaling with multiple clients (stagger close dates).

kpi-templates/ (5 files)

Use: When onboarding a client — pick the template for their industry

Industry-specific KPI scorecards with definitions, formulas, and benchmarks:

  • Service Business: Revenue per employee, gross margin, CAC, LTV, utilization, churn
  • Construction: Backlog, margin by job, bid-to-win, WIP vs billing, overhead rate
  • Medical/Dental: Revenue per provider, collections rate, visit volume, overhead ratio, AR by payer
  • Staffing: Gross margin per placement, fill rate, spread, time to fill, client concentration
  • Franchise: Revenue per location, same-store growth, labor %, four-wall EBITDA, royalty burden

CRM & Pipeline — Track Every Lead, Never Drop a Ball

This is how you stay organized without a complex CRM. When you hit 15+ leads, migrate to HubSpot (free). Until then, this works.

pipeline-tracker.md

Use: Daily — update as leads move through stages

9 pipeline stages from Lead In to Won/Lost. Active pipeline table with columns: Name, Company, Industry, Revenue Est., Source, Stage, Last Contact, Next Action, Notes. Plus a Lost/Passed tracker with reasons.

follow-up-cadence.md

Use: Every time you need to follow up with anyone

Exact messages for every stage: after connection accepted (Day 0), Day 3 value-add, Day 7-14 soft engagement, Day 21-30 soft ask for call. Post-proposal: Day 3, 7, 14 chase. Post-lost: graceful exit + 90-day revisit. Each touchpoint has 2-3 message variations so you don't sound robotic.

referral-partner-tracker.md

Use: Weekly — track referral relationships

Tracking table: Name, Company, Type (Attorney/Banker/Coach/CPA/Bookkeeper/Insurance/EOS), Last Contact, Referrals Sent, Referrals Received, Relationship Temperature, Next Action. Plus nurture cadence: monthly LinkedIn engagement, quarterly coffee, thank-you notes on wins.

weekly-pipeline-review.md

Use: Every Monday morning — 5 minutes

Quick checklist: How many leads came in? Where are deals stuck? Outstanding proposals over 7 days? Did I reach out to 2 referral partners? Pipeline value? Weekly goal setting.

lead-scoring-guide.md

Use: When evaluating any new lead

A-Leads (pursue immediately): $1-10M service business, CO-based, owner-operated, has bookkeeper, expressed pain, came from referral. B-Leads (nurture): right size wrong timing or geography. C-Leads (pass): pre-revenue, hourly only, price-shopping, red flags.

networking-event-playbook.md

Use: Before every BNI, Chamber, or networking event

Before: research attendees, prep 3 conversation starters. Your 10-second intro. Questions to ask others. After: same-day LinkedIn connect, 48-hour follow-up email, 1-week coffee invite for top connections. What NOT to do: don't pitch, don't hand out cards unprompted, listen 80%.

email-newsletter-template.md

Use: When ready to launch monthly newsletter (not urgent)

Monthly "CFO Insight" template: one insight (2-3 paragraphs), one client story (anonymized), one question to think about, CTA to book a call. Subject line formulas, tone guide, and launch checklist. Start with ConvertKit or Mailchimp free tier.

Excel Templates — QBO Data In, Analysis Out

Every Excel file has 3 types of tabs: INSTRUCTIONS (which QBO report to export), DATA_INPUT (paste here), and OUTPUT tabs (formatted analysis with formulas). Paste QBO data → everything calculates automatically.

cash-flow-forecast.xlsx

Use: Monthly for every Growth+ client — and during Diagnostic assessments

13-month rolling cash flow forecast. Paste QBO P&L by month into DATA_INPUT. FORECAST tab auto-builds: Revenue → COGS → Gross Profit → OpEx → Net Income → Cash Adjustments (AR/AP changes, debt, distributions, capex) → Net Cash Flow → Running Cash Balance. Month 13 forecasts using trailing 3-month average. Conditional formatting turns red if cash goes negative. Includes Best/Base/Worst scenario section.

monthly-financial-package.xlsx

Use: Monthly for every client — this is the core deliverable

Paste QBO P&L and Balance Sheet into input tabs. Auto-generates: Executive Summary (revenue, margin, net income, cash — all with MoM and YoY comparisons + conditional formatting), full P&L Analysis (10 columns: current, prior month, $ variance, % variance, prior year, YoY variance, YTD, budget, budget variance), Balance Sheet Analysis, and KPI Scorecard (10 metrics with status indicators).

kpi-dashboard.xlsx

Use: Monthly — maintain for Growth+ clients

Multi-industry KPI dashboard. Paste QBO data (P&L, BS, AR/AP aging). SETTINGS tab lets you set industry, employee count, and RAG thresholds. DASHBOARD auto-calculates 11 KPIs with Red/Amber/Green status: Revenue Growth, Gross Margin %, Net Margin %, OpEx Ratio, Current Ratio, Quick Ratio, AR Days, AP Days, Cash Conversion Cycle, Revenue per Employee, Overhead Rate.

pipeline-tracker.xlsx

Use: Daily — your CRM until you set up HubSpot

PIPELINE tab with dropdown data validation for Stage (9 options), Source (8 options), Industry. Conditional formatting by stage. DASHBOARD tab with COUNTIFS/SUMIFS: pipeline by stage, by source, conversion rates, won/lost this month, average deal size. REFERRAL_PARTNERS tab for tracking referral relationships.

pricing-calculator.xlsx

Use: Before sending any proposal — sanity check your pricing

Input estimated hours by activity (close review, package prep, calls, forecasting, KPIs, ad hoc, lender prep). Auto-calculates: total hours, effective hourly rate at each tier, recommended tier (IF formula), profitability analysis (revenue minus hours × your internal rate). COMPARISON tab shows all 4 tiers side-by-side with break-even analysis.
