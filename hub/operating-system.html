6. LEGAL & CONTRACT INFRASTRUCTURE ⚠️ PRIORITY

Status: NOT DONE — do this before taking another client.

You are operating without liability protection. This is the single highest-risk gap in the practice right now.

What You Need (Flat-Fee Attorney Engagement — $500-$1,500)

Master Service Agreement (MSA) - Defines the relationship, liability limits, IP ownership, confidentiality - Every client signs this once

Engagement Letter Template (one per tier) - Specific scope of work and deliverables for that tier - What’s included, what’s explicitly NOT included - Duration, renewal terms

Scope Creep Clause - Anything outside defined deliverables is either billed separately at $250/hr or requires a tier upgrade - This protects your time AND trains clients to respect boundaries

Payment Terms - Auto-pay via ACH, billed on the 1st, non-negotiable - No invoicing, no chasing. Payment is a system, not a conversation - Late payment = pause of services after 7 days

Termination Clause - 30-day minimum notice, both sides - Protects you from getting dropped mid-month after you’ve done the work

7. SALES PLAYBOOK — Discovery Call Framework

Before the Call (5 min prep)

  • Check HubSpot notes — how did they find you? What do you know?
  • Look up their business (website, LinkedIn, Google)
  • Know their industry basics

The Call (20 min intro / 45 min discovery)

Opening (2 min): > “Hey [Name], thanks for making time. Quick background — I’m a CPA and business operator here in Colorado. I run my own businesses and work as a fractional CFO for owners like you. But before I talk about me — tell me about what’s going on in your business.”

Let them talk. Listen for pain signals.

Discovery Questions (10-15 min) — ask 4-5 of these:

  1. “What’s keeping you up at night financially?” (Opens the emotional door — this is where the real pain lives)

  2. “Walk me through how you currently see your numbers. Monthly financials? Cash flow forecast? Who does your books?” (Reveals sophistication level and current gaps)

  3. “When you make a big decision — hiring, expanding, taking on debt — how do you decide? Gut? Spreadsheet? Advisor?” (Tells you if they have financial infrastructure or are winging it)

  4. “What’s your revenue and roughly how many employees?” (Qualifies size — you want $1M-$20M)

  5. “If you had a CFO sitting next to you right now, what would you ask them first?” (This is your scope — they’re telling you exactly what they need)

  6. “Have you worked with a fractional CFO or outsourced finance person before?” (Sets expectations — if yes, ask what worked/didn’t)

  7. “What does the next 12 months look like for you? Growing? Stabilizing? Selling?” (Identifies strategic context and urgency)

Transition (1-2 min): > “Based on what you’re telling me, here’s what I think is going on… [reflect their pain back in clear terms]. Here’s how I’d typically approach that…”

Light solution overview (3-5 min): - Don’t pitch. Describe how you’d work together. - “Month one would be getting my arms around your books and cash flow. By month two, you’d have a dashboard and forecast you can actually use. From there we work on the strategic stuff — pricing, margins, growth planning.” - Mention you’re a CPA — builds credibility without bragging

Close (2 min): > “I think there’s a real fit here. Here’s what I’d suggest — I’ll put together a simple proposal with scope and pricing, and send it over by [day]. You can take a look, ask questions, no pressure. Sound good?”

After the Call (within 24 hours)

“Hey [Name] — enjoyed our conversation today. As promised, I’ve attached a proposal that outlines how I’d approach [their specific pain]. Take a look and let me know if you have any questions. Happy to adjust scope if needed.”

Proposal Template

Keep it to one page. Structure:

  1. The Situation — 2-3 sentences reflecting what they told you (shows you listened)
  2. What I’ll Do — bullet list of specific deliverables for their business
  3. How It Works — monthly retainer, what’s included, cadence (e.g., “Monthly close, one strategy call per month, ad hoc access via email/text”)
  4. Investment — monthly fee, minimum engagement (suggest 3-month minimum)
  5. Next Step — “Reply to this email or book a time here: [Calendly]”

Pricing Guide

Tier Monthly Duration Includes Target Client
Diagnostic $1,500/mo 60-day engagement Financial assessment, gap analysis, cash flow snapshot, 2 strategy calls, written roadmap New prospects who need to see the value before committing. Designed to convert to Essential+
Essential $2,500/mo Ongoing Monthly close review, financials, 1 strategy call/mo, email support (48hr SLA) $1M-$3M revenue, simple operations
Growth $5,000/mo Ongoing Everything above + 13-month rolling forecast, KPI dashboard, 2 calls/mo, ad hoc access $3M-$10M, growing, more complexity
Strategic $7,500-$10,000/mo Ongoing Full fractional CFO — planning, board/investor prep, M&A support, weekly check-ins, same-day access $10M+, complex, high-stakes

The Diagnostic tier is a strategic door-opener. $1,500/mo for 60 days lowers the “yes” barrier for skeptical prospects. By the end, they’ve seen your work and the conversation shifts from “should I hire a fractional CFO?” to “which tier fits?” Expected conversion rate: 70%+.

Never quote hourly. You’re selling outcomes and access, not time. If they push on hourly, the retainer effectively works out to $150-$250/hr — but frame it as “this is what it costs to have a CFO on your team.”

Critical rule: Retainer includes defined deliverables per engagement letter. It does NOT include unlimited calls, texts, or bookkeeping tasks. Boundaries enforced by contract.

7. STAY IN YOUR LANE — Client Boundary Framework

What You DO:

  • CFO-level strategy, planning, and analysis
  • Cash flow management and forecasting
  • Financial reporting and dashboards owners can use
  • Pricing, margins, profitability analysis
  • Budgeting and scenario modeling
  • Tax planning and entity structuring (you’re a CPA)
  • M&A due diligence and support
  • Financial systems setup (QBO, etc.)

What You DON’T Do (and who to refer to):

They Need You Say Refer To
Day-to-day bookkeeping “I partner with bookkeepers — I can recommend one and oversee their work” Local bookkeeper (build this referral network)
Tax return preparation “I do tax planning, but I partner with a tax preparer for returns” Tax prep firm
Audit/assurance “That’s a different engagement — I can connect you” Audit firm
Legal/entity formation docs “I can advise on structure, but you need an attorney for the docs” Business attorney
IT/software implementation “I’ll spec what you need, my partners handle implementation” IT consultant
Insurance “I can analyze your coverage gaps, but you need a broker” Insurance broker
HR/payroll admin “I can help structure comp, but ongoing payroll admin isn’t my lane” Payroll service / HR consultant

The referral network IS the business model. Every bookkeeper, tax preparer, and attorney you partner with becomes a two-way referral source. Build this deliberately.

Scope Creep Defense

When a client asks for something outside your lane: > “That’s important and I want to make sure it gets done right. That’s not my core — but I know someone great. Let me connect you.”

When you feel yourself sliding into bookkeeping-level work: > “I can handle this short-term, but let’s get a bookkeeper in place so I can focus on the strategic work that actually moves the needle for you.”

Rule of thumb: If the task doesn’t require your CPA brain or operator experience, it’s not your task. Delegate or refer.
