The Operating Philosophy

You are one person building a firm. That means every minute matters. The goal isn't to work more hours — it's to make every hour count.

Three rules govern everything below:

1. Systems over willpower. You don't "remember" to follow up. The system tells you to. You don't "try" to post on LinkedIn. It's on the calendar. Decisions are made once (in the system), then executed repeatedly (by habit).

2. Revenue-generating activities first. Every morning, your first work should be something that could lead to money: outreach, discovery calls, proposals, follow-ups. Admin, bookkeeping, and optimization come second.

3. Axel does the building. You do the selling and delivering. If a task can be done by your AI agent, delegate it. Your time is worth $150-250/hr. Spend it on things only you can do: relationships, judgment calls, and client conversations.

📋 Daily Routine

~3-4 hours of focused work. Revenue activities before everything else.

8:00 AM

☕ Morning Scan (15 min)

Check email for client urgencies or prospect replies. Check LinkedIn notifications. Don't get sucked in — just scan for anything that needs immediate response.

8:15 AM

🎯 Revenue Block (60 min) — NON-NEGOTIABLE

This is the most important hour of your day. Pick from: send LinkedIn connection requests (5-10), follow up on warm leads, send proposals, reach out to referral partners, comment on 3-5 LinkedIn posts. Nothing else happens until this is done.

9:15 AM

📝 LinkedIn Post (15 min)

M/W/F only. Post is already drafted (by Axel). Review, edit if needed, post. Engage with 2-3 comments on other people's posts. Done.

9:30 AM

📞 Client Work & Calls (flexible)

Discovery calls, strategy calls, client deliverable work. This is your core — the work clients pay you for. Calendly controls the schedule.

12:00 PM

🍽️ Lunch + Break

Step away. You run a business, not a sweatshop. Come back fresh.

1:00 PM

📊 Admin & Delivery (90 min)

Update pipeline tracker. Prep financial packages. Build forecasts. Update KPI dashboards. Send follow-up emails. This is where your Excel templates and checklists live.

2:30 PM

🧠 Strategic Work (60 min)

Deeper thinking: refine your ICP, plan content, research prospects, work on the business (not just in it). Or delegate to Axel — "research this prospect," "draft this proposal," "prep me for tomorrow's call."

3:30 PM

📧 Email & Wrap-Up (30 min)

Respond to remaining emails. Send any pending follow-ups. Update pipeline with the day's activity. Set tomorrow's top 3 priorities. Close the laptop.

4:00 PM

🏠 Done.

Family time. You built this business for freedom, not to recreate a 9-5. Protect this boundary.

⚡ Automation & Efficiency Hacks

Things that save you 5+ hours per week.

1. Gmail Canned Responses = Instant Emails

Save these as Gmail templates right now: welcome email, proposal cover email, follow-up Day 3/7/14, not-a-fit pass, referral partner outreach, call summary. Two clicks to send instead of 15 minutes writing.

2. "Hey Axel" = Instant Delegation

Things to say to me daily:

• "Prep me for my call with [prospect] tomorrow — research their company"

• "Draft a proposal for [client] at the Growth tier"

• "Write a follow-up email for [prospect] — warm tone"

• "Draft this week's LinkedIn posts"

• "Update my pipeline — I had a discovery call with [name], moving to Proposal Sent"

If you can describe it in a sentence, I can do it.

3. Otter.ai on Every Call = No Note-Taking

Add Otter to your Google Meet. It transcribes automatically. After the call, skim the transcript, copy key points into your call summary email. You stay present on the call instead of scribbling notes.

4. Google Calendar Blocks = Autopilot Schedule

Block these as recurring events RIGHT NOW:

• Mon-Fri 8:00-9:15 AM → "Revenue Block" (color: red)

• Mon/Wed/Fri 9:15-9:30 AM → "LinkedIn Post" (color: blue)

• Mon 8:00-8:05 AM → "Weekly Pipeline Review"

• Fri 2:00-3:00 PM → "Weekly Review + Next Week Planning"

Your calendar tells you what to do. You just follow it.

5. QBO Export → Excel Template = 10 Minutes Per Client

The financial package that used to take 2 hours now takes this:

1. Export P&L from QBO (1 min)

2. Paste into monthly-financial-package.xlsx (1 min)

3. Formulas auto-calculate everything (0 min)

4. Review and add commentary (8 min)

Total: 10 minutes of actual work per client per month.

6. Duplicate, Don't Recreate

Every proposal, engagement letter, and deliverable starts from a template. Never start from a blank page. Duplicate the template, fill in the client name, customize 20%. Done.

7. Batch Similar Tasks

Don't: Write one email, then do a forecast, then write another email, then update LinkedIn.

Do: All emails at once. All forecasts at once. All LinkedIn at once. Context switching kills your efficiency.

8. The "End of Day 3" Rule

At the end of every day, write down your top 3 priorities for tomorrow. Not a to-do list — just THREE things. When you sit down in the morning, you already know what to do. Zero decision fatigue.
